Requirement Engineering For Generative AI Agents

Investigating how the requirement engineering phase changes when GenAI agents are involved into the process.

Motivation & Details

Generative AI agents are increasingly embedded in software products, not as isolated features, but as autonomous components that perceive context, make decisions, and take actions on behalf of users or the system. This shift raises an under-explored question: how does the requirement engineering (RE) process change when agents are part of the product being built?

This research focuses on understanding how agents are treated during the RE phase: how their goals and constraints are elicited from stakeholders, how their expected behavior is specified (and by whom), how non-functional concerns such as safety, reliability, and controllability are surfaced early, and how the introduction of agents changes the collaboration between engineers, product owners, and end users. We are particularly interested in the practices that emerge organically in teams shipping agent-based systems today.
