Software Engineering
for AI Systems
@ SeSa

The SE4AI research group at the University of Salerno investigates the challenges of engineering software systems that incorporate machine learning components. Our work spans testing, quality assurance, technical debt, and DevOps for AI-based applications.

Research Areas

📊

Empirical Software Engineering

Applying rigorous empirical methods — controlled experiments, case studies, and mining software repositories — to produce evidence-based insights on software development practices and tools.

🔄

Software Maintenance & Evolution

Studying how software systems change over time, with a focus on technical debt, code smells, refactoring, and the long-term sustainability of large-scale codebases.

🤖

Software Engineering for AI/ML Systems

Addressing the unique engineering challenges of AI-based systems, including quality assurance, testing, architectural design, and MLOps practices for machine learning pipelines.

🤝

Social & Human Factors in SE

Investigating how developers, teams, and organizations interact with software processes and tools, covering topics such as productivity, collaboration, diversity, and communication in hybrid teams.

🔐

Software Security & Testing

Developing techniques to detect vulnerabilities, assess software reliability, and improve test effectiveness through methods such as mutation testing, fuzzing, and static analysis.
